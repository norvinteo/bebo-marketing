Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to questions we often receive from new clients

What is your typical process for a new project?

Our process follows six key stages: Discover, Define, Design, Develop, Deploy, and Delight. We begin by understanding your business needs and goals, then define the project scope, create visual designs, build the technical components, implement the solution, and measure its success. Throughout this process, we maintain close communication with our clients to ensure the final product meets or exceeds expectations.

How long does a typical project take?

Project timelines vary depending on complexity and scope. A basic branding project might take 4-6 weeks, while a comprehensive website with custom functionality could take 8-12 weeks or more. During our initial consultation, we'll provide a more accurate timeline based on your specific project requirements.

What is your pricing structure?

We typically work on a project-based pricing model, providing a comprehensive quote after understanding your project's scope and requirements. For ongoing relationships, we also offer retainer packages with monthly billing. Our pricing reflects the value we bring to your business through our combined expertise in both design and systems development.

Do you offer ongoing support after project completion?

Yes, we offer various support options after project completion. For websites and systems, we provide maintenance packages that include regular updates, security monitoring, and technical support. For branding projects, we offer retainer services for ongoing design needs and brand management. We believe in building long-term relationships with our clients and supporting their continued success.

How involved will I be in the project?

Client collaboration is essential to our process. While we handle the heavy lifting of design and development, we involve you at key milestones for feedback and approval. This typically includes the initial discovery phase, concept review, design approval, and pre-launch testing. We strive to find the right balance—keeping you informed and involved without overwhelming you with technical details.
